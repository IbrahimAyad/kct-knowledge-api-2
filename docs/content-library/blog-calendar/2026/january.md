**What IS Velvet? (Fabric Science 101):**

**Definition:** Velvet is a woven tufted fabric with a **pile surface**—short, dense fibers standing upright creating soft, plush texture. Traditionally silk-based (luxury), modern velvet uses cotton or synthetic fibers (accessibility).

**Pile Construction:**
- **How it's made**: Two layers of fabric woven together, then cut apart to create raised pile
- **Pile height**: 1-3mm typically (shorter = more formal, longer = more casual)
- **Fiber content**: Silk velvet (most luxurious), cotton velvet (most common), synthetic velvet (budget-friendly)

**Why Pile Matters:**
- **Light interaction**: Pile catches and reflects light differently based on angle = **dimensional shimmer** (not flat like wool)
- **Directional nap**: Pile has direction (stroke one way = smooth, stroke opposite = rough) = unique visual depth
- **Warmth**: Pile traps air pockets = insulation (warmer than smooth wool)

**Velvet Warmth Properties for January:**

**Understanding Wool Weight (The Ounce System Explained):**

**What Does "Ounce" (oz) Mean?**
- **Definition**: Fabric weight measured in ounces per square yard of material
- **How it's measured**: Weight of one yard x one yard piece of fabric
- **Notation**: Written as "11 oz," "13 oz," "14 oz," etc.

**Why Weight Matters:**
- **Warmth**: Heavier = more fibers = more insulation
- **Drape**: Heavier = better structure and shape retention
- **Durability**: Heavier = more durable, longer-lasting
- **Seasonality**: Heavier = fall/winter, lighter = spring/summer
- **Formality**: Medium-heavy (11-13 oz) = most formal business/wedding weight

**Wool Weight Categories for January:**

| Weight Category | Ounces (oz) | Seasonality | Temperature Range | Best For |
|-----------------|-------------|-------------|-------------------|----------|
| Tropical/Summer | 7-9 oz | Spring/Summer | 70-90°F | Warm weather weddings |
| Four-Season | 9-11 oz | Year-round | 55-80°F | Versatile all-season |
| Medium-Heavy | 11-13 oz | Fall/Winter | 35-70°F | Standard winter suiting |
| Heavy | 13-15 oz | Winter | 20-65°F | Coldest winter weddings |
| Extra-Heavy | 15-18 oz+ | Extreme winter | 10-60°F | Outdoor winter, overcoats |

**Recommended for January Weddings:** **11-14 oz = sweet spot** (warm enough for winter, not too heavy for heated indoor venues)
