## FAQ Questions for Featured Snippets

1. **"What is the best suit color for college graduation?"**
   > Navy blue is the most versatile graduation suit color, working for the ceremony, job interviews, weddings, and business settings. Charcoal gray and medium gray are excellent alternatives.

2. **"What is tropical weight wool and is it good for summer weddings?"**
   > Tropical weight wool (7-9 oz) is specifically woven with an open, breathable structure for warm weather. It maintains a formal appearance while promoting airflow, making it ideal for summer weddings.

3. **"Does linen wrinkle too much for a wedding?"**
   > Linen naturally wrinkles, but many consider this part of its relaxed elegance. For crisper appearance, choose linen-cotton blends (70/30 or 60/40) that reduce wrinkling while retaining breathability.

4. **"What color linen suit is best for a summer wedding?"**
   > Natural beige, light gray, and tan are the most versatile linen suit colors. They reflect sunlight, photograph beautifully in natural light, and work across various wedding themes.

5. **"Can I get a wedding suit in one week?"**
   > Yes, using in-stock or quick-ship options combined with express alterations. Focus on off-the-rack suits with minimal tailoring needs—basic alterations (hem, sleeve length) can be completed in 48-72 hours.

6. **"What should groom wear if groomsmen wear suits?"**
   > The groom can distinguish himself through: (1) wearing a tuxedo while groomsmen wear suits, (2) wearing a different color within the same palette, (3) adding unique accessories, or (4) choosing a different lapel style.

## Seasonal Fabric Guide

### Temperature Reality by Region

| Region | Temperature | Recommendation |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| Southeast | 75-85°F | Linen or tropical wool mandatory |
| Southwest | 80-90°F+ | Linen primary |
| Northeast/Midwest | 70-78°F | Tropical wool or cotton ideal |
| Pacific Northwest | 65-75°F | Cotton or mid-weight wool works |

### Breathability Rankings (Most to Least)

1. Pure linen (exceptional airflow, moisture-wicking)
2. Linen-cotton blends (balance of breathability + structure)
3. Tropical weight wool 7-9 oz (natural temperature regulation)
4. Cotton (good breathability, moderate moisture retention)
5. Seersucker (puckered texture creates air pockets)
6. Wool-mohair blends (surprisingly cool)

### Construction Features for Summer

- **Unlined:** Maximum breathability, less structure, very casual
- **Half-lined:** Ideal balance (shoulders/chest structured, back ventilated)
- **Fully lined with summer-weight lining:** Traditional appearance, specialized lining
- **Butterfly back:** Seam construction allows movement and airflow
- **Higher armholes:** Reduces fabric bunching, improves mobility
